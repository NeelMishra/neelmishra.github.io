    Robust and Quantile Regression

    July 2026 · 16 min read

    Ordinary least squares is sensitive to outliers because squaring makes large residuals dominate the loss. Robust variants change the loss so one bad point cannot pull the whole line too far.

    Least Absolute Deviations

    Least absolute deviations minimizes $\sum_i |y_i-\hat y_i|$. It targets the conditional median rather than the conditional mean and is less sensitive to large errors.

    Huber Regression

    Huber loss is quadratic near zero and linear far away. Small residuals behave like least squares, while large residuals are downweighted:

    $$L_\delta(e)=\begin{cases}\frac12e^2,& |e|\le\delta\\ \delta(|e|-\frac12\delta),& |e|>\delta.\end{cases}$$

    Quantile Regression

    Quantile regression estimates a conditional quantile instead of the mean. The pinball loss for quantile $\tau$ penalizes underprediction and overprediction asymmetrically. It is useful for prediction intervals, risk estimates, and service-level forecasting.

    Choose the loss for the question. Squared error asks for the mean, absolute error asks for the median, and pinball loss asks for a chosen quantile.
