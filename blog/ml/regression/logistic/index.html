    Logistic Regression: Mastery RoadmapWIP

    Logistic Regression · Work in progress

    Work in progress. This roadmap page will link out to ten logistic regression topic pages as they are written, from the probability interpretation through interview derivations.

    Series Roadmap

    • Probability, Log-Odds, Decision Boundaries, and Interpretation - how logistic regression maps a linear score to a probability through log-odds, and how to interpret coefficients as changes in log-odds.
    • Maximum Likelihood and the Log-Loss Derivation - deriving the log-loss objective for logistic regression from maximum likelihood estimation over Bernoulli outcomes.
    • Gradient Descent, Newton's Method, and IRLS - optimizing logistic regression with gradient descent, Newton's method, and iteratively reweighted least squares, and how the three relate.
    • Regularization, Identifiability, and Perfect Separation - why perfectly separable data breaks unregularized logistic regression, and how L2 and L1 penalties restore identifiability.
    • Multiclass Logistic and Softmax Regression - extending logistic regression to more than two classes with one-vs-rest and softmax regression, and the cross-entropy loss that trains it.
    • Decision Thresholds, Class Imbalance, and Error Costs - choosing a decision threshold, handling class imbalance, and weighing false positive against false negative costs.
    • Confusion Matrix, ROC-AUC, PR-AUC, and Log-Loss - reading the confusion matrix, ROC-AUC, precision-recall AUC, and log-loss as complementary ways to evaluate a classifier.
    • Probability Calibration and Reliability - checking whether predicted probabilities are trustworthy with reliability diagrams, Platt scaling, and isotonic regression.
    • Diagnostics and Production Failure Modes - diagnostics for logistic regression fit quality and the common ways classification models silently fail in production.
    • Logistic Regression: Essential Interview Derivations - the logistic regression derivations most likely to appear in interviews, worked from first principles: the log-loss gradient, the Hessian, and the softmax generalization.
