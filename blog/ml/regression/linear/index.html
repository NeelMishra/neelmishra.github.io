    Linear Regression: Mastery RoadmapWIP

    Linear Regression · Work in progress

    Work in progress. This roadmap page will link out to twelve linear regression topic pages as they are written, from what the model estimates through interview derivations.

    Series Roadmap

    • What Linear Regression Estimates - what linear regression actually estimates: the conditional mean function, population parameters versus sample estimates, and the assumptions that make the estimate meaningful.
    • OLS, Normal Equations, and Projection Geometry - deriving the OLS estimator, the normal equations, and the projection-onto-column-space geometry behind least squares.
    • Gauss-Markov, Standard Errors, Tests, and Intervals - the Gauss-Markov theorem, standard errors of OLS coefficients, hypothesis tests, and confidence intervals for linear regression.
    • Multicollinearity, VIF, and the FWL Theorem - diagnosing multicollinearity with the variance inflation factor and understanding partialled-out effects through the Frisch-Waugh-Lovell theorem.
    • Model Specification, Transformations, and Interactions - choosing functional form: variable transformations, interaction terms, categorical encodings, and common specification errors.
    • Solving Least Squares Reliably: Cholesky, QR, and SVD - comparing Cholesky, QR, and SVD based solvers for least squares, and why numerical stability matters more than raw speed.
    • Gradient Descent for Linear Regression - batch, stochastic, and mini-batch gradient descent for linear regression, learning rate behavior, and when to prefer iterative solvers over closed form.
    • Ridge, Lasso, and Elastic Net - ridge, Lasso, and Elastic Net as penalized least squares, their bias-variance tradeoffs, and a Bayesian reading of each penalty.
    • Robust and Quantile Regression - least absolute deviations, Huber regression, and quantile regression for when outliers or non-mean targets break ordinary least squares.
    • Residual Diagnostics, Influence, and Evaluation Metrics - reading residual plots, leverage and Cook's distance for influence, and evaluation metrics like R-squared, adjusted R-squared, and RMSE.
    • Linear Regression Production Failure Modes - common ways linear regression breaks in production: data drift, extrapolation, leakage, and silent violations of modeling assumptions.
    • Linear Regression: Essential Interview Derivations - the linear regression derivations most likely to appear in interviews, worked from first principles: normal equations, bias-variance decomposition, and ridge closed form.
