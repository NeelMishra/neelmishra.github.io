    Bayesian Regression

    July 2026 · 17 min read

    Bayesian regression treats unknown coefficients and variance parameters as random variables and updates prior beliefs with a likelihood. The result is a posterior distribution, not only a point estimate. Prediction integrates parameter uncertainty through the posterior predictive distribution.

    $$p(\beta,\sigma^2\mid y,X)\propto p(y\mid X,\beta,\sigma^2)p(\beta,\sigma^2).$$

    Exact One-Parameter Conjugate Update

    The three states combine prior $\beta\sim N(0,4)$ with likelihood summary $\hat\beta\mid\beta\sim N(\beta,1)$ at $\hat\beta=2$, yielding posterior $N(1.6,0.8)$.

    Gaussian Conjugacy

    With $y\mid\beta\sim N(X\beta,\sigma^2I)$, known $\sigma^2$, and $\beta\sim N(m_0,V_0)$, the posterior is Gaussian:

    $$V_n^{-1}=V_0^{-1}+X^TX/\sigma^2,\qquad m_n=V_n(V_0^{-1}m_0+X^Ty/\sigma^2).$$

    Posterior precision is prior precision plus data precision. A zero-mean isotropic Gaussian prior produces ridge-like shrinkage in the posterior mode. A Laplace prior corresponds to a lasso-like posterior mode, although the full posterior contains more information than the mode.

    Posterior Prediction and Intervals

    For a new row $x_*$, parameter uncertainty contributes $x_*^TV_nx_*$ and observation noise contributes $\sigma^2$. A predictive interval is therefore wider than an interval for the conditional mean. Bayesian credible intervals express posterior probability under the model and prior; frequentist confidence intervals have a repeated-sampling interpretation.

    Hierarchical Regression and Partial Pooling

    Group-specific coefficients can share a population distribution. Partial pooling shrinks noisy groups toward a common mean while allowing data-rich groups more independence. Complete pooling ignores group variation, and no pooling estimates every group separately. Exchangeability and the group-level prior are substantive assumptions.

    A weakly informative prior rules out implausible scales while leaving substantial likelihood influence. An informative prior encodes defensible external evidence. A hierarchical prior estimates shared scale and location parameters across related coefficients or groups. Prior predictive checks distinguish these choices by examining the outcomes they imply before observed responses enter the fit.

    Computation and Diagnostics

    Conjugate models permit analytic updates. More complex models use Laplace approximations, variational inference, or Markov chain Monte Carlo. MCMC diagnostics include multiple chains, effective sample size, rank-based convergence statistics, autocorrelation, and divergent-transition checks. Posterior predictive checks compare replicated data with features of the observed data.

    Interview trap. A prior does not make a misspecified likelihood harmless. Posterior concentration can become confidently wrong when dependence, support, censoring, or the mean structure is incorrect.

    Bayesian logistic regression is not conjugate under an ordinary Gaussian coefficient prior. Laplace approximation, variational inference, MCMC, or a specialized augmentation is required. Proper weakly informative priors can keep separation from sending coefficient mass toward unrealistic scales, but posterior geometry and predictive calibration still require checks.

    Worked Scalar Update

    A coefficient estimate with likelihood $N(2,1)$ and prior $N(0,4)$ has prior precision $0.25$ and data precision $1$. The posterior variance is $1/1.25=0.8$, and the posterior mean is $(0.25\cdot0+1\cdot2)/1.25=1.6$. Shrinkage moves the estimate toward the prior mean while reducing variance.

    Modeling Safeguards

    • Prior predictive simulation reveals impossible scales before fitting.
    • Sensitivity analysis checks whether conclusions depend on plausible prior alternatives.
    • Weakly informative priors can regularize separation and poorly identified coefficients.
    • Cross-validation and predictive checks answer different questions and are complementary.
    • Data-dependent priors require a coherent empirical-Bayes or hierarchical account.

    Frequentist regularization connections appear in ridge, lasso, and elastic net. Logistic separation and prior stabilization appear in regularization and separation.

    Interview Quick Checks

    PromptComplete answer
    Posterior mode versus full posterior?The mode is one high-density parameter value. The full posterior represents uncertainty, dependence, asymmetry, and predictive integration.
    Why does a Gaussian prior resemble ridge?The negative log prior is quadratic, so maximizing the posterior adds an L2 penalty to the negative log likelihood.
    What validates a Bayesian model?Prior predictive checks, posterior predictive checks, sensitivity analysis, and computational diagnostics address distinct failure modes.
    What is partial pooling?Group estimates share population-level information, with shrinkage determined by within-group information and estimated between-group variation.
