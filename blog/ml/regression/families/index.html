    Regression Types for Interviews

    July 2026 · 16 min read

    Regression is a family of models, not only ordinary least squares. Model choice starts with the quantity being estimated and the kind of outcome being observed. A real-valued amount, binary event, count, positive duration, and partly observed event time need different assumptions.

    The exact target is called the estimand. It might be a conditional mean, a median, another quantile, an event rate, or a survival probability. The possible values of the outcome are its support. Naming these two items prevents many model-choice mistakes.

    For models of a conditional mean, a common template is

    $$g\big(E[Y\mid X]\big)=\eta(X).$$

    The link $g$ connects the mean to a predictor $\eta(X)$. A polynomial or spline changes the shape of $\eta$. A generalized linear model can change the outcome distribution and link. WLS and GLS change how errors are weighted or correlated. Quantile and survival models use different targets, so they do not fit this mean template directly.

    Five Parts of a Regression Choice

    ChoicePlain-language questionExamples
    Outcome and targetWhat can the outcome be, and which part of it matters?Mean amount, binary probability, count rate, quantile, survival probability
    Relationship shapeIs the relationship straight, curved, grouped, or interactive?Linear terms, interactions, polynomials, splines, additive smooths
    Fitting ruleWhat rule chooses the coefficients?Least squares, weighted least squares, likelihood, quantile loss, posterior updating
    Error patternDo errors have unequal spread or depend on one another?Constant variance, inverse-variance weights, GLS, cluster-robust covariance
    Complexity controlHow are unstable or overly flexible estimates restrained?Ridge, lasso, smoothing penalties, Gaussian and hierarchical priors

    These choices are separate. The quadratic model $\beta_0+\beta_1x+\beta_2x^2$ changes the relationship shape, but it is still linear in the coefficients. OLS can estimate those coefficients. Polynomial regression is therefore not an alternative fitting rule to OLS; it is a different set of columns for OLS to fit.

    Which Part of the Model Must Change?

    Each state shows evidence for a different change: relationship shape, error pattern, outcome family, pooling, representation, or censoring-aware likelihood.

    A Selection Framework for Interview Cases

    Observed clueLikely starting pointMain condition to check
    A continuous outcome follows a smooth curvePolynomial basis, spline, or GAMHeld-out error improves without unstable extrapolation
    Residual spread changes or nearby rows share errorsWLS or GLSThe proposed weights or dependence pattern are credible
    The outcome is binary, a count, or strictly positiveGLMThe family, link, and mean-variance relationship fit the outcome
    Related groups have little data individuallyHierarchical Bayesian regressionPartial pooling across groups is scientifically reasonable
    Many predictors carry overlapping informationPCR or PLSComponent count is chosen inside validation
    Some event times are not fully observedCox, parametric survival, or AFTThe censoring and model-specific time assumptions are credible

    A Complete Answer Structure

    A clear model recommendation covers six parts:

    1. Target: the mean, quantile, event rate, hazard, survival probability, or full predictive distribution.
    2. Outcome values: real-valued, binary, count, positive, ordinal, or censored.
    3. Relationship: straight, curved, additive, interactive, grouped, or low-dimensional.
    4. Fitting method: least squares, weighted least squares, likelihood, partial likelihood, or posterior computation.
    5. Assumptions: independent errors, correct family, credible covariance, non-informative censoring, or exchangeable groups.
    6. Validation: split design, tuning boundaries, diagnostics, uncertainty, and deployment range.
    One change does not repair every layer. A flexible curve does not fix dependent errors, leakage, biased censoring, or unsupported extrapolation. Each problem needs a matching remedy.

    PriorityExpected coverage

    Recurring Interview Traps

    • Outcome type narrows the choices but does not determine relationship shape, error dependence, or validation.
    • Unequal error variance does not by itself bias OLS coefficients when $E[\varepsilon\mid X]=0$, but ordinary standard errors become wrong.
    • Many zeros do not by themselves prove that a zero-inflated model is needed.
    • Polynomial degree, spline flexibility, and component count are tuning choices and belong inside validation.
    • Hazard ratios, odds ratios, rate ratios, and time ratios have different meanings.
