    Regression Types for Interviews

    July 2026 · 16 min read

    Regression interviews test more than ordinary least squares. Strong answers begin with the estimand, identify the support and dependence structure of the response, state the assumptions behind estimation and inference, and separate model flexibility from evaluation discipline. This focused series organizes those decisions around eight pages while retaining the deeper linear regression and logistic regression foundations.

    The recurring contract is $g(E[Y\mid X])=\eta(X)$, where the response distribution, link $g$, and predictor structure $\eta$ are separate choices. Polynomial terms, splines, and latent components change the predictor structure. Weighted and generalized least squares change the covariance model. Generalized linear models change the response family and link. Bayesian methods change the inferential framework. Survival methods change the likelihood because some event times are censored.

    Five Independent Axes

    AxisQuestionRepresentative choices
    Outcome distributionWhat values can $Y$ take, and how does variance depend on its mean?Gaussian, Bernoulli, Poisson, negative binomial, Gamma, censored time
    Functional formHow does $X$ determine the target?Linear terms, interactions, polynomial bases, splines, additive smooths, latent scores
    Loss or estimatorWhich target and fitting principle are used?OLS, WLS, maximum likelihood, partial likelihood, posterior updating, quantile loss
    Error covarianceAre residual scales unequal or residuals dependent?Identity covariance, inverse-variance weights, GLS, cluster-robust or sandwich covariance
    Prior or regularizationHow is weak identification or excess flexibility controlled?Ridge, lasso, smoothing penalties, Gaussian or hierarchical priors

    A quadratic model $\beta_0+\beta_1x+\beta_2x^2$ is a polynomial feature specification and remains linear in its coefficients. OLS is an estimator that can fit that specification. Calling quadratic regression a replacement for OLS confuses the functional-form axis with the estimator axis.

    Step, Play, Reset Model Selection

    A Complete Answer Structure

    1. Target: conditional mean, quantile, event hazard, survival probability, or full posterior predictive distribution.
    2. Support: real-valued, binary, count, positive, ordinal, or censored response.
    3. Structure: linear effects, basis expansion, additive smooths, interactions, or latent components.
    4. Estimation: least squares, weighted least squares, maximum likelihood, partial likelihood, or posterior computation.
    5. Assumptions: exogeneity, rank, covariance structure, response family, censoring mechanism, or prior exchangeability.
    6. Validation: split strategy, tuning boundary, diagnostics, uncertainty target, and deployment support.
    Interview distinction. A flexible mean function does not automatically repair invalid standard errors, dependence, leakage, censoring bias, or extrapolation. Each failure belongs to a different layer of the model contract.

    Interview Priority Map

    PriorityExpected coverage
    Must knowSimple and multiple OLS, assumptions, logistic regression, regularization, residual diagnostics, feature leakage, and train-validation-test separation
    Common extensionsPolynomial terms, interactions, WLS, count GLMs, splines, robust and quantile losses, and cross-validated tuning
    SpecializedGLS covariance models, Bayesian hierarchy, PCR and PLS, zero-inflated counts, GAM interactions, and censored survival likelihoods

    Recurring Interview Traps

    • Outcome support alone does not choose a complete model; mean structure, covariance, and deployment target still matter.
    • Heteroskedasticity does not by itself bias exogenous OLS coefficients, although classical standard errors fail.
    • Many observed zeros do not prove zero inflation, and overdispersion does not identify its cause.
    • High polynomial degree, many spline knots, or many latent components must be tuned inside validation folds.
    • A hazard ratio, odds ratio, rate ratio, and time ratio answer different questions.

    Focused Series Roadmap

    Polynomial and Basis Regression

    Linear models in transformed features, hierarchy, conditioning, orthogonal bases, regularization, and extrapolation.

    Weighted and Generalized Least Squares

    Heteroskedasticity, correlated errors, whitening, feasible GLS, sandwich inference, and efficiency.

    Generalized Linear Models

    Exponential-family likelihoods, links, variance functions, IRLS, deviance, and family selection.

    Splines and GAMs

    Local bases, knots, natural constraints, smoothing penalties, effective degrees of freedom, and additive effects.

    Bayesian Regression

    Prior-likelihood updating, conjugacy, shrinkage, hierarchical pooling, posterior prediction, and diagnostics.

    PCR and PLS

    SVD scores, supervised latent components, standardization, component selection, interpretation, and leakage.

    Censored and Survival Regression

    Survival and hazard functions, censoring likelihoods, Kaplan-Meier, Cox regression, AFT models, and evaluation.
