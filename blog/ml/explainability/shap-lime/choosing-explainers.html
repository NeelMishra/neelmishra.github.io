    Choosing SHAP, LIME, and Other Explainers

    July 2026 · 15 min read

    SHAP and LIME are not interchangeable buttons. They answer related but different questions, and both depend on choices outside the trained model. A good explainability workflow starts with the question: is the goal debugging, compliance, user-facing explanation, feature audit, or scientific insight?

    Decision Table

    Need Prefer Reason
    Fast local explanation for a black-box model LIME Weighted local surrogates are simple and can be implemented with ordinary regression.
    Additive attributions that sum to prediction minus baseline SHAP Local accuracy is built into the Shapley formulation.
    Tree model explanations at scale Tree SHAP Tree structure gives exact or efficient shortcuts unavailable to generic Kernel SHAP.
    Debugging broad feature reliance Permutation importance first It is simpler and often enough to catch leakage or dominant shortcut features.

    Common Pitfalls

    • Unrealistic perturbations: masking or sampling can create rows outside the data distribution.
    • Correlated features: attribution may be split across interchangeable features in unintuitive ways.
    • Background dependence: SHAP baselines change when the background dataset changes.
    • False causality: feature attribution describes model behavior, not real-world intervention effects.
    Recommended workflow. Start with permutation importance for global sanity checks, use LIME for quick local debugging, and use SHAP when additive accounting and stable attribution semantics are worth the extra cost.
