    BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers for Language Understanding

    Devlin et al. · 2019 · NAACL

    Rough draft. This page is a stub from the “Recommended Papers” scaffold. Expect a proper, structured write-up — problem, method, results, what makes it special — in a future revision.

    Authors: Jacob Devlin, Ming-Wei Chang, Kenton Lee, Kristina Toutanova

    Year: 2019

    Venue: NAACL

    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805

    Why this paper matters

    The paper that defined “modern NLP” for several years. Cast every classification task as fine-tuning a single pretrained Transformer encoder, set state of the art across the GLUE board, and made pretraining + fine-tuning the default research workflow.

    The core idea

    Pretrain a Transformer encoder on two unsupervised objectives: masked language modeling (predict randomly masked tokens given bidirectional context) and next-sentence prediction. Then fine-tune on labelled downstream tasks with a small task head.
