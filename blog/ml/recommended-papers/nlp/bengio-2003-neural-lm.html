    A Neural Probabilistic Language Model

    Bengio et al. · 2003 · JMLR

    Authors: Yoshua Bengio, Réjean Ducharme, Pascal Vincent, Christian Jauvin

    Year: 2003

    Venue: JMLR

    Link: https://www.jmlr.org/papers/volume3/bengio03a/bengio03a.pdf

    Why this paper matters

    The first neural language model. Before this paper, language models were n-grams with smoothing. Bengio et al. proposed learning continuous word embeddings and feeding them through an MLP to predict the next word — the recipe every modern LM is a descendant of.

    The core idea

    Replace discrete n-gram counts with learned word embeddings + a feedforward neural network that produces a probability distribution over the next word. The shared embedding table generalises across rare contexts that an n-gram never sees.
