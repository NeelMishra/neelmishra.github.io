    Neural Machine Translation of Rare Words with Subword Units

    Sennrich, Haddow & Birch · 2016 · ACL

    Rough draft. This page is a stub from the “Recommended Papers” scaffold. Expect a proper, structured write-up — problem, method, results, what makes it special — in a future revision.

    Authors: Rico Sennrich, Barry Haddow, Alexandra Birch

    Year: 2016

    Venue: ACL

    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1508.07909

    Why this paper matters

    The paper that brought byte-pair encoding to NLP. BPE remains the tokenizer of choice for GPT-2/3/4, Llama, Mistral, and most modern frontier LMs. The CS336 lecture's tokenization section is largely a tour of this idea.

    The core idea

    Greedy bottom-up merge algorithm: start with single bytes/characters, count the most frequent adjacent pair, merge it into a new token, repeat until vocabulary size hits a target. Encoder applies the learned merges in order to new text. Bounded vocabulary, perfect byte-level fallback, good compression.
