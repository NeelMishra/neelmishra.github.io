    Prediction and Entropy of Printed English

    Shannon · 1950 (published 1951) · Bell System Technical Journal

    Rough draft. This page is a stub from the “Recommended Papers” scaffold. Expect a proper, structured write-up — problem, method, results, what makes it special — in a future revision.

    Authors: Claude E. Shannon

    Year: 1950 (published 1951)

    Venue: Bell System Technical Journal

    Link: https://www.princeton.edu/~wbialek/rome/refs/shannon_51.pdf

    Why this paper matters

    This is the paper that invented the language model. Shannon framed English as a stochastic source, defined its entropy, and ran human-prediction experiments to estimate it. Every probabilistic language model since — from n-grams to GPT — is doing a version of what Shannon proposed here.

    The core idea

    Estimate the entropy of English at the character and word level by asking humans to guess the next character given a prefix, and measuring the distribution of guesses needed. Bounds on the entropy of English fall out of the experiment.
