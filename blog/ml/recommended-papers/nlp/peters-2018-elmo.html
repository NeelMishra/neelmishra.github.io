    Deep Contextualized Word Representations

    Peters et al. · 2018 · NAACL

    Rough draft. This page is a stub from the “Recommended Papers” scaffold. Expect a proper, structured write-up — problem, method, results, what makes it special — in a future revision.

    Authors: Matthew E. Peters, Mark Neumann, Mohit Iyyer, Matt Gardner, Christopher Clark, Kenton Lee, Luke Zettlemoyer

    Year: 2018

    Venue: NAACL

    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1802.05365

    Why this paper matters

    ELMo proved the “pretrain a deep language model, then plug it into downstream tasks” recipe before BERT made it canonical. The first crisp demonstration that contextual word embeddings dominate static ones (word2vec, GloVe).

    The core idea

    Pretrain a bidirectional LSTM language model on a large corpus; for each downstream task, compute a weighted combination of the LSTM's hidden states as the contextual word representation, and feed that into a task-specific head.
