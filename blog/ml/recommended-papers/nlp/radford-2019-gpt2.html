    Language Models are Unsupervised Multitask Learners

    Radford et al. · 2019 · OpenAI Technical Report

    Rough draft. This page is a stub from the “Recommended Papers” scaffold. Expect a proper, structured write-up — problem, method, results, what makes it special — in a future revision.

    Authors: Alec Radford, Jeffrey Wu, Rewon Child, David Luan, Dario Amodei, Ilya Sutskever

    Year: 2019

    Venue: OpenAI Technical Report

    Link: https://cdn.openai.com/better-language-models/language_models_are_unsupervised_multitask_learners.pdf

    Why this paper matters

    The first crisp demonstration that a large enough autoregressive Transformer trained on web text can do zero-shot task transfer — no fine-tuning required. The intellectual bridge from BERT-style pretraining to GPT-3 in-context learning.

    The core idea

    Scale up GPT (decoder-only Transformer trained left-to-right on next-token prediction) to 1.5B parameters, train it on WebText (a curated crawl), and evaluate it on standard benchmarks by simply prompting it with task descriptions — no task-specific fine-tuning.
