    Neural Machine Translation by Jointly Learning to Align and Translate

    Bahdanau et al. · 2015 · ICLR

    Rough draft. This page is a stub from the “Recommended Papers” scaffold. Expect a proper, structured write-up — problem, method, results, what makes it special — in a future revision.

    Authors: Dzmitry Bahdanau, KyungHyun Cho, Yoshua Bengio

    Year: 2015

    Venue: ICLR

    Link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/1409.0473.pdf

    Why this paper matters

    Introduced attention — the mechanism the entire Transformer architecture is built from. The single-vector bottleneck of vanilla seq2seq vanishes once the decoder can soft-attend to every encoder state at every step.

    The core idea

    Instead of forcing the encoder to compress the source into one vector, expose the entire encoder hidden-state sequence to the decoder, and let the decoder learn a soft alignment (attention weights) between each output position and the source positions.
