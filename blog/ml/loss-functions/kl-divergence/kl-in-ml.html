    KL Divergence in ML Objectives

    July 2026 · 16 min read

    KL divergence appears whenever a model distribution is pushed toward a reference distribution. It is not just a diagnostic number; it is often part of the training objective.

    Classification

    For one-hot labels, minimizing cross-entropy is equivalent to minimizing KL from the empirical target distribution to the predicted distribution, up to a constant entropy term. With soft labels, the same formula trains the model to match the whole target distribution.

    Distillation

    Knowledge distillation often minimizes KL between a teacher distribution and a student distribution. The student is not only told the correct class; it is told how the teacher distributes probability across plausible alternatives.

    Variational Autoencoders

    A VAE objective contains a reconstruction term and a KL regularizer. The KL term keeps the approximate posterior close to the prior, making latent samples usable at generation time.

    Policy Constraints

    In reinforcement learning, KL can constrain a new policy so it does not move too far from an old policy in one update. This turns KL into a stability control.
