    Earth Mover's Distance Foundations

    July 2026 · 17 min read

    Earth Mover's Distance, also called Wasserstein-1 distance, measures how much work is needed to move one distribution into another. "Work" means mass moved times distance moved. Unlike KL and JS, EMD cares about the geometry of the space.

    ▶ Moving Mass to Match a Target

    Orange mass must be moved to match the green target. Moving mass farther costs more.

    Why It Is Different

    KL and JS compare probability assigned to matching bins. EMD asks how far mass must travel. If two image distributions shift by a few pixels, EMD can recognize that the distributions are close geometrically, while bin-wise divergences may treat them as very different.

    Geometry is the point. EMD needs a ground distance between outcomes. Without a meaningful distance, the transport cost is not meaningful.
